Nubank

Nubank is a Brazilian bank with just over 10 years in the market that has revolutionized the country’s financial sector. It was the first fully digital bank and the first to offer a credit card without bureaucracy. Its launch campaign focused on "Nu", which means "naked", emphasizing transparency and the absence of the fine print that traditional banks use to hide fees and special conditions.

The big idea

To position Nubank, a fully digital bank, as a humanized institution focused on people and their life moments.

While some banks use staged photos or illustrations, I chose to highlight real people of different ages and ethnicities, showing that the bank is for everyone and present in all life stages. The visual identity follows a clean design, with generous white space to reinforce the brand’s transparency. The purple accents reference the credit card, the well-known "roxinho" ("litte purple").

Product improvements

The homepage was simplified, with strong headlines that lead to internal pages with more details. Since people might question whether the account is truly free, I emphasized this point in the first two sections. Another common concern is the lack of physical branches, so the last section highlights how Nubank leverages technology to ensure service security.

Measuring Impact

If this version of the site were live, I would track conversion rates on the new homepage and clicks on the PJ and Ultravioleta segment links. Additionally, an email survey could assess customer perception, measuring whether they feel the bank is more humanized and secure.
