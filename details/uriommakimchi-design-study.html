Uri Omma Kimchi

Uri Omma Kimchi is a family-owned grocery store and restaurant dedicated to sharing authentic Korean cuisine. Their signature product, kimchi, is a family recipe passed down through generations.

Big Idea

Highlighting the handmade and family-driven essence already present in the brand and restaurant, making learning about Korean dishes and spices easy and fun.

I drew a lot of inspiration from the paper liner that comes with the trays at the restaurant—it features doodle-style illustrations that explain each dish in a simple, engaging way. Additionally, their social media includes a mascot, Kimchito, a doodle of a napa cabbage, reinforcing the brand’s need to communicate in a lighthearted and playful way.

Digital strategy

Uri Omma Kimchi, in addition to their Instagram, has two websites. The first is their official site, which showcases the restaurant’s menu and, secondarily, a bit of the brand’s story. The second is a third-party platform used for delivery, which also features the menu and product catalog. Due to internal processes, I understand they lack the structure for an independent e-commerce with their own delivery system. Therefore, my proposal was to create a clearer distinction between the official website and the delivery platform. For the official site, I suggested focusing the homepage on the brand’s story and presenting the dishes in an educational way. This idea stemmed from a demand I noticed on Instagram, where many users don’t understand the names of the dishes or know what they are.

Measuring Impact

There are many questions on their social media that could be answered through the website. I believe one way to evaluate the website’s success would be to check if the number of user inquiries has decreased and if people are spending more time reading the information available on the site. Instagram could also emphasize the website as a guide to better understand the dishes.
