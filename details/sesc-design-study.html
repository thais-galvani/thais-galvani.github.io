SESC

SESC is a Brazilian institution that offers a wide range of programs, including in-person courses in craftsmanship, painting, sculpture, and drawing, as well as film screenings, theater plays, concerts, sports facilities, libraries, and lodging options across the country.

The big idea

Bringing the tangible and physical aspects of SESC into the digital space, creating an interface that feels material and real.

I drew inspiration from editorial layouts, natural textures, printed program materials, event posters, building signage, and films. The color palette features softer tones that evoke the look of ink printed on recycled paper, reinforcing the sense of materiality.

Product improvements

One of the key enhancements in navigation was the restructuring of categories based on the five senses and user interests: Hearing-focused events (concerts and live music) were grouped into Musicals & Shows; Theater and cinema, both highly visual experiences, were combined into a single category; Hands-on workshops (crafting, painting, sculpture) were grouped under Workshops & DIY, emphasizing tactile engagement; Physical activities like volleyball, basketball, swimming, dance, and yoga were consolidated into a Sports category. By simplifying the categories, users are encouraged to explore new activities based on their interests, rather than sticking to predefined sections.

Measuring Impact

To evaluate whether these improvements are effective, key metrics would include time spent exploring the site, click-through rates, heatmaps of key sections, and conversion rates (how often users sign up for activities). Additionally, tracking engagement with social sharing buttons can indicate how much users appreciate and recommend the content.
